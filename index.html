Who I Help

You might be ready for counseling if…

You're starting a GLP-1

You want long-term success — not just weight management, but a healthier relationship with food and yourself.

You're already on medication

The medication is working, but something emotional or psychological still feels unresolved.

You're dealing with food noise

Obsessive thoughts about food, rituals, or rigidity have entered your life and you want support.

You have a complex food history

Years of dieting or emotional eating that predate the medication — therapy helps you heal that history.

You feel alone in this

There's no one you can truly talk to about the weight, the shame, and the complexity. You want someone who gets it.

You're thinking about stopping

You want the mindset and habits in place so your progress stays yours — with or without the medication.

My Approach

Evidence-based, compassion-led

I draw from a range of evidence-based therapies, always tailoring my approach to what fits you. No two GLP-1 journeys are alike — and neither is our work together.

Click any therapy below to learn how it supports your journey.

Cognitive Behavioral Therapy
CBT
+
CBT helps you identify and reframe the automatic thoughts that drive emotional eating and self-criticism. We examine patterns like "I already ruined it, I may as well keep eating" and replace them with more flexible thinking — so your mind supports your goals instead of working against them.
Motivational Interviewing
MI
+
Motivational Interviewing strengthens your own motivation to change by exploring your values, ambivalence, and readiness. Rather than telling you what to do, I help you discover what genuinely drives you — so that healthier habits come from within and stick long-term.
Acceptance & Commitment Therapy
ACT
+
ACT helps you stop fighting difficult thoughts and feelings, and instead act in alignment with what truly matters to you. For GLP-1 clients, this means building psychological flexibility — accepting setbacks without shame, and continuing to move toward a life defined by your values, not your weight.
Mindfulness-Based Approaches
Mindfulness
+
Mindfulness practices help you develop present-moment awareness of hunger, fullness, emotions, and urges — without reacting automatically. This is especially powerful for food noise: learning to observe cravings with curiosity rather than control, and to respond intentionally rather than impulsively.
Somatic & Body-Focused Approaches
Somatic
+
Weight and body image aren't just psychological — they live in the body too. Somatic approaches help you reconnect with physical sensations, recognize stress responses, and process stored emotional experiences that can drive overeating or self-criticism. This supports deeper, more embodied healing.

Are sessions in person, virtual, or both?

I offer telehealth sessions through SimplePractice, a HIPAA-compliant video platform. Sessions are conducted via secure video and are available to clients in Washington DC, Maryland, and many additional states through the Social Work Licensure Compact.

In-person sessions may also be available — please inquire when you reach out.

What are your fees?

Please reach out directly for current session rates. I'm happy to discuss fees, session frequency, and what to expect financially before you make any commitment.

Payment is due at the time of each session. I accept credit cards and other standard payment methods.

Do you accept insurance?

I do not accept insurance. By operating outside of insurance, I'm able to offer you more personalized, flexible care without the limitations that insurance often imposes — including restrictions on type, frequency, and duration of treatment.

This also means greater confidentiality: I am not required to share your information with any insurance company, and no formal diagnosis is required to begin working together.

You may still be reimbursed. As a licensed out-of-network provider, I can provide a superbill — a detailed receipt — that you can submit to your insurance company for potential out-of-network reimbursement. I recommend calling the member services number on your insurance card and asking:

  • "Do I have out-of-network mental health benefits?"
  • "What is my out-of-network deductible?"
  • "What percentage is reimbursed once the deductible is met?"

I'm happy to help you navigate this process — just ask.

Where can I access telehealth with you?

I am licensed in Washington DC (LICSW) and Maryland (LCSW-C), and I serve clients in many additional states through the Social Work Licensure Compact — an interstate agreement that allows licensed social workers to practice across participating states.

The list of compact states continues to grow. Please reach out with your state of residence and I'll confirm whether I can see you there. All telehealth is conducted through SimplePractice, a secure, HIPAA-compliant platform.

Is therapy confidential?

Yes. Everything shared in our sessions is strictly confidential. I will never disclose your information to family, employers, or others without your written consent.

There are limited legal exceptions — such as imminent risk of harm to yourself or others, suspected abuse, or a court order. These are explained fully in the consent documents you'll complete before our first session.

What is the cancellation policy?

I ask for at least 24 hours notice to cancel or reschedule. I understand life is unpredictable and am happy to discuss any extenuating circumstances.

Late cancellations or no-shows may be subject to a fee.
